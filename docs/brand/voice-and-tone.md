# Souso voice and tone

Souso talks like a calm, capable sous chef who has your back. It takes the
weekly "what's for dinner?" load off you and never makes you feel behind.

## Principles

1. **Warm and plain.** Short, human sentences. Say the thing. "Groceries,
   sorted." beats "Optimise your weekly grocery workflow."
2. **Calm, never pushy.** We suggest, we don't nag. The week is quiet by
   default; the AI sits behind one gentle "Ask Souso" prompt.
3. **Reward, never guilt.** Especially around money and tips: no-tip is a real,
   unpunished choice. We're honest (an optional fee is called a fee, not a tip,
   when a default is on).
4. **Confident, low-key.** We know food and the shop. We don't shout, over-claim,
   or pile on exclamation marks.
5. **You stay in control.** Souso fills the basket; you check out. The copy never
   implies it buys for you.

## Sounds like

- "Groceries, sorted."
- "Tell Souso how your household eats once. Every week of dinners just works,
  basket and all."
- "no more 'what's for dinner?'" (a Schoolbell note)
- "set once, done"
- "Swipe a dish to swap it."
- "6 ingredients reused, nothing left over."

## Not this

- AI-tell filler: delve, leverage, robust, seamless, unlock, elevate, empower,
  streamline, "in today's fast-paced world".
- Em-dashes or en-dashes. Use commas, colons, full stops, or parentheses.
- Guilt or pressure: "Don't miss out", "You forgot to...", sad mascot faces.
- Rhetorical-reversal filler: "it's not just X, it's Y".
- Gratuitous tricolons (three parallel items where one or two do).

## Mechanics

- Sentence case for headings and buttons (not Title Case, not ALL CAPS, except
  the small olive eyebrow labels which are uppercase-tracked).
- Prices in euro, Dutch format (`€2,80`). Dutch-first market; default recipe
  copy is English with a one-tap Dutch switch.
- Numbers as numerals in UI ("25 min", "540 kcal", "2 adults").
- Buttons are verbs: "Get started", "Make my cart", "Cook", "Add a dinner".
